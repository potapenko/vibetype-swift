# HoldType 1.0.5

This release restores reliable macOS recording behavior while keeping the
longer-session and recovery improvements from the withdrawn 1.0.4 release.

## Changes

- Choose a maximum recording length from 1 to 15 minutes in Behavior settings.
  The default remains five minutes, and changes apply to the next recording.
- Reaching the selected limit now finishes the recording normally and continues
  transcription instead of rejecting the completed audio.
- The floating recording indicator and menu bar show a countdown during the
  final 15 seconds before the selected limit.
- Completed recordings are protected in History before transcription begins, so
  interrupted or failed attempts can be played, retried, or deleted without
  recording them again.

## Fixed

- Restored reliable Right Command hold-to-record behavior: recording remains
  active until the key is physically released and finishes exactly once.
- Fixed the floating recording indicator restarting its animation on duration
  updates; it now appears promptly and animates continuously while recording.
- Made finalization reliable when the recorder, shortcut release, and duration
  limit finish at nearly the same time.
- Preserved non-empty recordings across app interruption or termination and
  made recovered recordings available from History after relaunch.
